What is All-on-X？

"All-on-X" refers to a dental implant procedure that replaces a full arch of missing teeth with a fixed bridge supported by a minimum of four to six dental implants. The "X" signifies the number of implants used (e.g., All-on-4, All-on-6), and the implants are strategically placed, with angled back implants often used to maximize bone support and avoid certain anatomical structures. This technique provides a stable, permanent, and natural-looking solution that restores a patient's smile and function, often in a shorter timeframe than traditional methods.

  • Reduction surgery: Extensive bone grafting is usually not required, so it is less invasive
  • Shorter Treatment Time: Compared to traditional dental implants, which require multiple surgeries and a lengthy wait, All-on-X implants can typically be completed more quickly.
  • More Secure: They provide a more secure bite and chewing experience than traditional removable dentures.
  • More Esthetically Pleasing: Missing teeth in the aesthetic area can be addressed more quickly, avoiding awkward periods.
